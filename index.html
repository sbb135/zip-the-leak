THE 8 LEAKS

Why patients still can't count on insurance when they get sick

  1. High copaysWhen patients are hit with high copays for appropriate treatments, insurance isn't really insuring them. Patients already paid premiums so they could afford care when they need it.
  2. DeductiblesHigh deductibles force sick patients to pay large sums out of pocket before coverage truly begins. That is not affordability. That is insurance failing at the moment it is supposed to work.
  3. CoinsuranceCoinsurance makes patients pay a percentage of a drug's list price, even when rebates later lower the net price. Patients get exposed to the inflated sticker price while others pocket the difference.
  4. Rebate-driven pricingPBMs and plans can profit from high list prices and large rebates, while patients do not get the rebate at the pharmacy counter. That keeps out-of-pocket costs high for the people unlucky enough to be sick.
  5. Copay accumulatorsWhen assistance from manufacturers does not count toward a patient's deductible or out-of-pocket maximum, patients can get hit twice. Help that was supposed to keep treatment affordable turns into a temporary illusion.
  6. Insurance that fails when patients need it mostReal insurance spreads costs across all of us through premiums so patients can afford appropriate treatments when illness strikes. Plans that shift costs back onto sick patients break that basic promise.
  7. Price controls that weaken innovationPatients need affordability, but they also need medicines to exist in the first place. If government overrides market-based pricing too early, it can make the next medicine not worth financing.
  8. Delayed genericizationSociety gets value from branded drugs when they go generic without undue delay. When that process is stalled, patients and payers remain stuck paying branded prices longer than they should.
