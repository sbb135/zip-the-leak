THE 8 LEAKS

Insurance design failures that delay treatment

  1. High copaysEven after paying premiums, patients face costs high enough to delay filling prescriptions or starting treatment.
  2. DeductiblesPatients delay care because they have to pay too much up front before coverage kicks in.
  3. CoinsurancePatients pay a percentage of every treatment cost, creating unpredictable and often unaffordable bills.
  4. PBM rebatesPharmacy benefit managers negotiate rebates that don't get passed to patients, keeping out-of-pocket costs high.
  5. Copay accumulatorsManufacturer assistance no longer counts toward deductibles, leaving patients with sudden cost cliffs mid-year.
  6. Broken insurance designPlans structured to shift costs to patients rather than provide meaningful coverage when it matters most.
  7. Price controls that weaken innovationPolicies that suppress drug prices can reduce incentives to develop treatments for the patients who need them most.
  8. Delayed genericizationWhen generic alternatives are slow to reach the market, patients stay locked into higher-cost branded options longer.
